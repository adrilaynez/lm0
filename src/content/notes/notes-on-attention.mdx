Attention is not passive. It's not just receiving. It's a form of action. [[attention-is-all-you-need]] explores this at the level of neural architecture.

When you attend to something, you're saying: this matters. This is worth my cognitive resources. This is why [[why-i-write]] — writing is where I direct my deepest attention.

Over time, what you attend to becomes what you are. Your mind is shaped by its attention patterns. [[the-shape-of-a-thought]] shows how attention literally shapes the shape of ideas.

This is why social media is so dangerous. Not because it's full of bad information. It's dangerous because it hijacks attention. It makes you attend to things that don't serve you.

And you can't think yourself out of it. Thinking requires attention. If your attention is already gone, you're stuck. [[writing-is-a-search-procedure]] requires reclaiming your attention.

The practice of sustained attention is meditation. The practice of chosen attention is reading. The practice of creative attention is writing.

All of these are practices of freedom — and freedom from what [[the-stub-problem]] calls the paralysis of incomplete ideas.
