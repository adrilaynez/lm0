A thought, before it is written, is a **possibility cloud**. It collapses
into a specific shape only when you commit it to language.

This is why writing is a tool for thinking, not a transcription of it. The
moment of commitment forces the cloud to choose a basin of attraction.

I keep returning to this idea because it explains why I cannot reliably
*remember* what I think before I write it down. There was no "it" to
remember — just a region of probability that hadn't yet been sampled.
